# Writing an Effective Neuroscience Lesson

## Photography and Cinematography

While the main purpose of a lesson is pedagogical, and clarity of what is being
visualized is critical, this atlas is capable of creating beautiful animations
and stills. Things that are beautiful are memorable. This captures their
attention and helps them learn when done correctly.

1. Use the entire viewport window. Do not waste space.
2. Balance between removing distractors and providing a rich visual experience.
3. Follow photography and cinematography compositional best practices.
4. Use artistic principles and techniques to draw the user's eye to what is important.

## Platform

Learn the full capabilities of the platform and spend the effort to use it to
its fullest extent to achieve the desired learning effect.

## Teaching

Follow best-practice teaching principles.

1. Follow a logical order and structure to the presentation.
2. Create, understand, and execute your teaching objectives.
3. Stay focused, but be generous with the informtion you provide. Share and
explain interesting details.
4. Offer context on how your lesson relates to peoples' lives.
5. Systems understanding in nueroscience is crucial. Offer systems context,
especially when designing visualizations.
6. In general, offer some historical context for the lesson.
    - What is the brief history of how knowledge evolved up to its current state?
    - Are there any interesting stories or biographical details worth sharing?
    - Curate and offer some current interesting research on the topic.
7. Cover the topic thoroughly at a upper division collegiate or postgraduate level.

## Other Best Practices

1. Write and copyedit your lesson with a clear writing style, (e.g., Strunk and White).
2. Provide and check the correctness and relevance of citations.
3. Search for and offer high-quality and free public web resources that might be
available to explore the topic more.
