# Model-fidelity disclosure design

- **Status:** Approved
- **Date:** 2026-07-21
- **Owner Bead:** `brain-atlas-yum.3`
- **Approval:** `brain-atlas-6nn`
- **Current-state authority:** `docs/SCIENTIFIC_TRACEABILITY.md`
- **Contract/runtime owners:** `brain-atlas-zmq.4` and later implementation Beads

## Goal

Let learners answer “what is data, what is modeled, and what should I not infer?”
without interrupting spatial learning. Scientific status stays quiet in the primary
hierarchy, remains discoverable beside the active visual, and opens in one explicit
action without changing the lesson or scene.

## Users and moments of need

- A first-time learner needs a short status before interpreting motion as anatomy or
  physiology.
- A domain-aware learner needs methods, assumptions, uncertainty, limitations, and
  citations without leaving the active scene.
- A lesson author needs reusable curated records rather than copying scientific prose
  into each lesson.
- A reviewer needs every claim to resolve to current traceability and source records.

The disclosure must work in guided lessons, free exploration, imported lessons,
compact/touch layouts, keyboard-only use, reduced motion, and no-WebGL fallback.

## Options considered

1. **Global methods page only:** simple, but detached from the representation being
   interpreted and too easy to miss.
2. **Badges or popovers on every object:** contextual, but visually noisy, repetitive,
   and likely to compete with anatomy.
3. **Layered progressive disclosure (approved):** one persistent stage-level control,
   separate concise geometry/activity statuses, one contextual note when a category
   first matters, and a detailed panel or sheet on demand.

Option 3 balances discoverability, context, and reading continuity.

## Status vocabulary

Status is descriptive, not a numeric confidence score or a trust grade. Records may
combine terms; a single badge must not flatten mixed representations.

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Data-derived** | Geometry or metadata originates in an identified dataset or atlas. |
| **Derived** | Processing such as meshing, tracking, resampling, decimation, or classification materially shaped the displayed result. |
| **Mirrored** | One side was generated from the other rather than independently observed. |
| **Modeled** | Behavior or direction is generated by an explicit algorithm and assumptions. |
| **Schematic** | Geometry or relationships are hand-authored for teaching rather than measured. |
| **Illustrative** | Display timing, amplitude, styling, or other behavior is not a measurement. |
| **Display-only** | Camera, clipping, material, or lighting changes presentation but not source anatomy. |

Concise status always separates at least these axes:

```text
Geometry: Data-derived · Derived
Activity: Modeled · Illustrative timing
```

If no activity exists, say **Activity: None** rather than omitting the axis. Terms
always have a plain-language explanation in the detailed disclosure.

## Curated record semantics

`brain-atlas-zmq.4` will define the exact schema, but each referenced fidelity record
must be able to express:

- a stable ID and represented subject (entity, layer, pathway, or activity model);
- geometry classifications, source dataset, processing/derivation summary, methods,
  and citations;
- activity classifications, direction basis, algorithm/parameter status, assumptions,
  and citations;
- supported interpretation in plain language;
- uncertainty and unsupported inferences;
- material known limitations;
- relevant license/source links; and
- review/version metadata so stale records can be detected.

Lesson files reference curated fidelity IDs. They may add scene-specific teaching
copy, but cannot redefine dataset provenance, representation status, or citations.
Unknown IDs and incompatible record versions fail validation rather than silently
appearing trustworthy. A genuinely unknown current-state fact is labelled
**Unknown/not verified**, not guessed.

## Progressive surfaces

### Persistent stage status

- Place a quiet **Model & sources** control beside the active visual or fallback.
- Show separate one-line geometry and activity statuses when space permits; compact
  layouts may place them inside the control's accessible description.
- Keep status available during lessons and Explore without taking focus or changing
  the scene.

### Contextual lesson note

When a representation category first affects interpretation, a lesson may show one
short note such as “Association direction is modeled, not measured by tractography.”
Do not repeat the same warning in every scene. The note links to the same detailed
record and remains ordinary readable lesson content in no-WebGL mode.

### Detailed disclosure

One click, tap, or keyboard activation opens these sections in order:

1. **What is shown** — represented layer/entity and concise geometry/activity status.
2. **Geometry and processing** — dataset, derivation, bilateral/mirroring status.
3. **Activity or direction model** — biological basis versus modeled/illustrative parts.
4. **What this supports** — defensible teaching interpretation.
5. **Assumptions and uncertainty** — evidence gaps without invented precision.
6. **Known limitations** — only material limitations under the rule below.
7. **Sources and licenses** — verified citations and applicable terms.

Wide layouts use a nonmodal panel that leaves the active scene and prose usable.
Compact layouts use a bottom sheet or full-screen inspector with focus containment.
Closing restores focus to **Model & sources**. Opening, closing, or following an
external citation never mutates lesson position, camera, filters, or playback.

## Materiality rule for known limitations

Show a limitation in the user-facing record when at least one is true:

- it could materially change anatomical or physiological interpretation;
- it affects confidence in alignment, laterality, direction, completeness, or source
  identity;
- the display could otherwise be mistaken for measured subject-level anatomy or
  physiology; or
- a higher-fidelity replacement is planned and the current approximation matters to
  intended use.

Do not expose internal Bead IDs, engineering chores, or harmless implementation
choices. Describe the user-visible consequence and, where useful, whether the record
is under review. Internal work ownership remains in Beads and
`docs/SCIENTIFIC_TRACEABILITY.md`.

## States and failure behavior

| State | Required behavior |
|---|---|
| Ready | Separate geometry/activity statuses and one-step detail access are present. |
| No activity | Explicit **Activity: None** status; no empty model section. |
| Record loading | Preserve the status/control footprint and announce loading without blocking lesson prose. |
| Record unavailable | Say status is unavailable; do not substitute a reassuring default. Built-in required records failing to load are an application error. |
| Imported unknown ID/version | Reject activation with line/field diagnostics while preserving the active lesson and source text. |
| Citation unavailable/offline | Keep citation identity and disclosure content visible; external navigation failure does not close the panel or lose scene context. |
| Reduced motion | Show the same model record beside the settled representation. |
| WebGL unavailable | Show the same statuses, notes, limitations, and sources beside the scene summary/fallback. |

## Accessibility and responsive behavior

- **Model & sources** is a semantic button or native disclosure control with a visible
  focus indicator and an accessible name that includes the active subject when useful.
- Status never relies on color alone. Terms remain text, not icon-only badges.
- Wide panels are nonmodal; compact sheets contain focus while open, support Escape
  and an explicit Close action, and restore focus to the invoker.
- Touch selection does not open citation-length content accidentally; detail opening
  is explicit.
- Scene changes announce the new scene, not every unchanged fidelity status. A changed
  material status is included in the scene's accessible description.
- Content remains readable at 200% zoom and with increased text spacing; long source
  lists scroll inside the disclosure rather than causing horizontal page scrolling.

## Behavioral scenarios

### Separate mixed representation status

```gherkin
Given a scene uses data-derived association contours with modeled impulse activity
When the scene becomes active
Then geometry is identified as data-derived and derived
And activity is identified separately as modeled with illustrative timing
And neither status is presented as measured axonal polarity or physiology
```

### Keyboard disclosure and focus restoration

```gherkin
Given keyboard focus is on “Model & sources” for the active scene
When the learner activates the control
Then the detailed record opens without changing scene state
And its headings, limitations, and source links are keyboard reachable
When the learner closes the record
Then focus returns to “Model & sources”
```

### Reject an unknown imported fidelity record

```gherkin
Given a valid lesson is active
And an imported lesson references an unknown fidelity record on line 18
When the learner validates the import
Then the active lesson and scene remain unchanged
And the source text is preserved
And an error identifies line 18 and the unknown fidelity ID
```

### No-WebGL parity

```gherkin
Given WebGL is unavailable
When a scene with modeled activity is active
Then its settled scene summary remains readable
And separate geometry and activity statuses remain available
And assumptions, material limitations, sources, and licenses open in one action
```

## Verification and documentation

- Pure schema/state tests validate required axes, status vocabulary, references,
  versions, and material limitation records.
- Browser tests cover wide/compact opening, focus containment/restoration, scene-state
  preservation, reduced motion, no-WebGL fallback, touch intent, zoom, and overflow.
- Human review checks prominence, warning fatigue, anatomical legibility, plain
  language, and whether the materiality rule produces useful rather than exhaustive
  warnings.
- Runtime scientific changes update the curated record,
  `docs/SCIENTIFIC_TRACEABILITY.md`, relevant license/citation files, public summaries,
  and UI disclosure atomically under the repository documentation lifecycle.

The current association-only disclosure in `index.html` is a validated prototype,
not the final generalized lesson component. Its separate geometry/direction/timing
copy and native disclosure behavior should be preserved while the contract becomes
data-driven.
